Understanding

Problem

Many girls between the ages of 8-16 enjoy comics and are starved for content that is representative of them. Although, in comic spaces they risk torment and have difficulty finding age appropriate content.

Insight

Women self-identify as comic book fans and regularly attend comic conventions but have difficulty accessing relatable content. Current digital comic platforms do not encourage discovery for children. They are not the primary audience therefore it is difficult for parents to find and monitor content that is age appropriate.

Solution

Panelle helps girls access age appropriate comics digitally. Panelle does this this and that

Competitive analysis

  • Most publishing companies exclusively distribute their own content.              
  • Children are not their primary target audience.
  • Popular competitors concentrated on how viewers would see and read each comic panel.

Reflection

Early on in the project my teammate and I collaborated equally. We evaluated each step in the process together and individually executed certain tasks like the paper prototypes and the early information architecture. Other processes were completely collaborative like creating the high fidelity prototype using Figma’s collaborative technology. When I continued the project independently, I refined the competitive analysis, information architecture, logo, and design system. I also redesigned the high fidelity prototype.

Although, if I were to continue to explore I would further my learning in accessible technology.  I recognize that it is important to consider that some older adults have arthritis and might not be able to gesture the symbols.  I would examine nonverbal communication methods.
